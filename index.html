MYP design cycle

What is the MYP Design Cycle, Anyway? The Design Cycle is
comprised of four steps: Inquiring and Analyzing, Developing
Ideas, Creating a Solution and Evaluating.
The four steps do not necessarily
have to be in the same order (VERY RARELY).










1 - Inquiring and Analyzing

In "Inquiring and Analyzing", you ask questions. A LOT of questions.
example: "What do you think the color of our product should be?"
or "Do you think an oval shape or a circle would be better?"

Then you analyze the feedback the people gave and
base your product off that.









2 - Developing Ideas

In "Developing Ideas", you think of different ways to design your product.
using the preferances from "Inquiring and Analyzing", you can create
blueprints and plans to create your product.









3 - Creating a Solution

In "Creating a Solution", its time to physically CREATE your product.
Using the analysis from Criterion 1 (Inquiring and Analyzing) and the plan from Criterion 2.
This is usually the most interesting stage of the project.









4 - Evaluating

"Evaluating" is the FINAL stage of your project. You have to distrubute your project
to other people and ask them to rate it. With the feedback that you got, you can either
leave the project or MAKE IT BETTER.
